    Frequently Asked Questions

    The Weather Archive is a web application that allows you to browse and download historical weather data from weather stations across British Columbia. You can access monthly observation data in CSV format for analysis and research purposes.

    1. Click on a weather station marker on the map, or select one from the "Weather Stations" list
    2. The Observation Data panel will open on the right side
    3. Select a year from the dropdown menu
    4. Click the download icon next to the month you want to download
    5. The CSV file will download to your computer

    All weather observation data is provided in CSV (Comma-Separated Values) format. This format can be opened in spreadsheet applications like Microsoft Excel, Google Sheets, or imported into data analysis tools.

    Click the "Weather Stations" button in the top-left corner of the map to open the station list. Use the search box at the top of the list to filter stations by name. You can also browse the map and click on any station marker directly.

    The available years vary by weather station. When you select a station, the year dropdown will show only the years for which data is available for that specific station.

    • Make sure JavaScript is enabled in your browser
    • Try refreshing the page
    • Clear your browser cache and reload
    • Try using a different browser (Chrome, Firefox, Safari, or Edge recommended)
    • Check your internet connection

    Note: Not all observations will be present at a given station, nor are all fields present in a given report. Only valid data will be displayed in the report.
    Disclaimer: Although reasonable care has been exercised in collecting and assembling these data, the Ministry does not warrant its accuracy and reliability.

    Manual Weather Stations

    Field Name/Definition Notes Units
    CODE Station Code
    DATE TIME Date and time of observation
    OBS TYPE Observation Type Standard Observations are taken twice daily. In the morning between 0530-0730hrs and in the evening between 1530-1800hrs.

    Interval observations are made upon request if weather conditions warrant extra readings.
    SKY COND Sky Condition CL = clear, OV = overcast, BR = broken, SC = scattered, OB = obscured, CL/H = clear/haze, SC/H = scattered/haze, BR/H = broken/haze
    PRCP TYPE Precipitation Type NIL = none, * = snow, R = rain, */R = snow/rain, FR = freezing rain
    PRCP RATE Precipitation Rate For Snow: VL = very light (<1 cm/hr), L = light (1 cm/hr), M = moderate (2 cm/hr), H = heavy (>3 cm/hr)
    For Rain: VL = very light (<1 mm/hr), L = light (1 mm/hr), M = moderate (2 mm/hr), H = heavy (>3 mm/hr)     		Snow in cm, Rain in mm
    MAX TEMP Maximum temperature since the last standard observation Degrees C
    PRES TEMP Present temperature at the time of the observation Degrees C
    MIN TEMP Minimum temperature since the last standard observation Degrees C
    THERM TRND Thermograph Trends FR = Falling rapidly (>5 deg in last 3 hours), FS = Falling slowly (1-5 deg in last 3 hours), ST = Steady (<1 deg in last 3 hours), RS = Rising slowly (1-5 deg in last 3 hours), RR = Rising rapidly (>5 deg in last 3 hours)
    RH Relative Humidity Percent
    NEW SNOW This is the accumulation of newly fallen snow since the last standard observation cm
    INT SNOW Interval Snow - Amount of snow that has accumulated on the interval snow board cm
    STORM SNOW Storm Snow - The amount (rounded cm) of snow since the beginning of a snow storm that has accumulated on a storm snow board cm
    STORM CLEARED Flag indicating that the Storm Board was cleared. This indicates that the observer felt that the storm was over.
    SNOW PACK This is the current depth of snow cm
    SURF COND Surface Condition SH = surface hoar, IC = ice crust, WE = wind effect, New = new, Oth = other, Old = old, Sl = slush
    PRCP GAUGE Precipitation Gauge - The current total depth of water contained in a precipitation gauge, irrespective of the form of the precipitation mm
    OLD GAUGE Old Gauge - Precipitation total as read from previous "Precipitation Gauge Total" reading mm
    NEW PRCP New Precipitation - The total precipitation, irrespective of form, to fall since the last standard observation (in mm). This is the difference between the current Precipitation Gauge Total and the Precipitation Gauge Total at the last standard observation (Precipitation Gauge Total Old). Minus sign is included if it is a negative value. mm
    FOOT PEN Foot Penetration - The total depth of water contained in a precipitation gauge, irrespective of the form of the precipitation, recorded at the last weather observation
    WND SPD EST Wind Speed Estimated C = calm, L = light, M = moderate, S = strong, E = extreme
    WND SPD MEAS Wind Speed Measured Km/Hr
    WND DIR Wind Direction Degrees N
    ATM PRESSURE Atmospheric Pressure mmHg
    PRESS TREND Atmospheric Pressure Trend FR = Falling rapidly (>5 deg in last 3 hours), FS = Falling slowly (1-5 deg in last 3 hours), ST = Steady (<1 deg in last 3 hours), RS = Rising slowly (1-5 deg in last 3 hours), RR = Rising rapidly (>5 deg in last 3 hours)
    AVAL OBS Avalanche Observed
    AVAL ON RD Avalanche on Road Observed
